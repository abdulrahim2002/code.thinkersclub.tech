      3 Discussion and Modelling:
      4 
      5     The number of characters in a string are all characters excluding the terminating character '\0' character which marks the end of string.
      6 In a genric defination of characters blanks, tabs and '\n' are not considered characters, but in Computer Science defination (more specifically ASCII character set)
      7 blank characters are also considered characters. For this implementation we will count these (blanlks, tabs and new-lines) characters.
      8 
      9 Algorithm:
     10 
     11 step 1: declare the iterating variable outside of iterating loop say 'i' and a "character_counter" variable.
     12 step 2: iterate through the loop until '\0' character is reached.
     13 step 3: at the end of iterating loop, i contains (number of characteres + 1, since it includes '\0' character).
     14 step 4: assign character_counter= i - 1 (to exclude terminating character).
     15 step 5: return character_counter.

     20 
     21 Discussion and Modelling:
     22 
     23 Word detection: 
     24     All words are usually seprated by one or more white space character or slash charaters. These characters are blanks, tabs, newlines slash and backslashes.
     25 while listing values in a text words may even be seprated by commas','.
     26 
     27     Hence, words can be sepreated by blank(' ') or tab(\t), slash('/')or backslash('\'), comma(',').
     28 So in general, we can iterate throuhgh the text to find a word seprating character. ones we find this character we know that a word has been read.
     29 so we iterate the word counter. This poses a problem, what if word-seprating-characters appear contigously.
     30 
     31     To solve this program we introduce the variable state which keeps track that the last word was part of text of a word-seprating-character.
     32 
     33 There are few more considerations:
     34 
     35 Words seprated by comma:
     36     The common text formatting ritual follows that you should seprate different words with space when using comma.
     37         eg. I'm like cars, computers, football and technology.
     38    
     39     Observe how cars and computers are seprated. SO in this case (cars,) is considered a word.
     40 
     41 words at the end of text:
     42 consider the last line of the text. It may be : And hence, we should not burn crackers.
     43 
     44 Note theat the last word is ended by a dot and the eof appears, hence there is a need to include '.' in word seprating characters list.
     45 It is less likely that these sentences will end in '?' , '!' although this is a valid exception.
     46 
     47 Algorithm:
     48 
     49 In word counting algorithm we use a variable "state". Which tells weather current character is IN or OUT of a word while iterating through the text.
     50 
     51 Cases:
     52 Now while iterating through the loop the following cases may arrise.
     53     case 1: current character is word-seprating-character.
     54         In this case there are two subcases.
     55         
     56             subcase 1: state is out.
     57             subcase 2: state is in.
     58     
     59     case 2: character is not word seprating.
     60         subcases: state is IN or OUT 
     61 
     62 step 1: declare a variable state, initialise state to OUT.
     63 step 2: iterate through the text. if a blank character (blank, tab or newline) or slash character(slash and black)
     64         if (current character is word seprating)
     65             if(state is out)
     66                 skip this iteration
     67             else
     68                 state = OUT
     69                 increment word counter
     70 
     71         if(current character is not word seprating)
     72             if(state is OUT)
     73                 state = IN
     74             else 
     75                 continue to next character
     76 step 3: return word counter.

     81 Discussion and Modelling:
     82  A sentence starts when another sentence ends or if it is the beginning of text, and is ended by ‘.’ , ’?’ or ‘!’.
     83 Hence, each sentence must contain 1 of these sentense-terminating characters which marks the end of current line.
     84 So simply counting the number of these sentence-terminating characters will give the number of sentences.
     85 
     86 Algorithm:
     87 Step 1: Declare and initialize a sentense_counter variable to 0. 
     88 Step 2: iterate through the text. if a sentence terminating character is encountered increment the sentense-counter.
     89 step 3: return sentense_counter.

     94 Discussion and modelling:
     95  A paragraph is a coherent block of text, such as a group of related sentences that develop a single topic or a coherent part of a larger topic.
     96 
     97 The beginning of a paragraph is indicated by
     98 •	the beginning of the content, that is, the paragraph is the first content in the document, or
     99 •	exactly one blank line preceding the paragraph text.
    100 
    101 The end of a paragraph is indicated by
    102 •	the end of the content, that is, the paragraph is the last content in the document, or
    103 •	one or more blank lines following the paragraph text
    104 
    105 A blank line contains zero or more non-printing characters, such as space or tab, followed by a new line.
    106 
    107 For our purposes we assume that each blank line contains a newline character only.
    108 
    109 So, based on the understanding that two paragraphs will be seprated by two or more new line charachters. And all non-empty text contain
    110 atleast 1 paragraph.
    111 
    112 Algorithm:
    113 step 1: initialise a variable paragraph counter to 1.
    114 step 2: iterate through the text until a new line character '\n' is found.
    115 step 3: if '\n' is found fetch another character, if it is also '\n', then we know that the 2 new lines are seprating 2 paragraphs.
    116         On each iteration check for terminatin character.
    117 
    118 step 4: increment paragraph-counter, iterate until the fetched character is not '\n', this eliminates subsequent '\n' from being checked.
    119 
    120 step 5: at the end of loop return paragraph-counter.

    211 Abstract: 
    212     A cell of an energy storage device with at least one electrode that is tabless, and methods of forming thereof, are
    213 described. The cell includes a first substrate having a first coating disposed thereon, wherein a second portion of the first substrate
    214 at a proximal end along the width of the first substrate comprises a conductive material. An inner separator is disposed over the first
    215 substrate. A second substrate is disposed over the inner separator. The second substrate has a second coating disposed thereon. The first
    216 substrate, the inner separator, and the second substrate in a successive manner, the first substrate, the inner separator, and the second
    217 substrate are rolled about a central axis.
    218 
    219 Description of the Related Art:
    220     Many types of battery cells are currently used as energy sources in electric
    221 vehicles and energy-storage applications. Current cells use a jelly-roll design in which the
    222 cathode, anode, and separators are rolled together and have a cathode tab and an anode tab to
    223 connect to the positive and negative terminals of the cell can. The path of the current
    224 necessarily travels through these tabs to connectors on the outside of the battery cell.
    225 However, ohmic resistance is increased with distance when current must travel all the way
    226 along the cathode or anode to the tab and out of the cell. Furthermore, because the tabs are
    227 additional components, they increase costs and present manufacturing challenges.
